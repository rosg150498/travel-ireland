Phoneix Park (Dublin)

The Phoneix Park is a beautiful park in Dublin, Ireland that can be found to the west of the city centre and to the north of the famous river Liffey.The Phoneix Park is open to all age groups and is an excellent destination for a day out with friends or family, or event a solo excursion for learning and reflection.

Dublin Zoo, located in Phoneix Park, was established in 1830 and was opened to the public on September 1, 1831. It holds to more than 700 species and tropical birds throughout the world.

In addition to the Papal Cross and Monuments, the Visitor Centre at Phoenix Park and Ash town Castle, the People’s Gardens, the Deerfield Residence, and the Magazine Fort can all be found in Phoenix Park. Once you get there, there will be a lot more to see.

Titantic Quarter (Belfast)

Until 1995, Queen’s Island in Belfast Harbour was home to the world’s largest waterfront regeneration project. It also features educational facilities, apartments, film studios and historical landmarks. On any given day, approximately 20,000 people live, work and visitors stay here at the Titanic Quarter.

Building Belfast’s future amid the awe-inspiring heritage of Belfast’s maritime and industrial past is the Titanic Quarter, which is a dynamic and buzzing destination on the Maritime Mile and is well worth visiting!

Peace Bridge (Derry/Londonderry)

After only three years since its opening the bridge has been enthusiastically received by the community and brought about a significant shift in the way that people think about the city. The Peace Bridge has become a focal point in city activities and events such as Park Runs, New Year celebrations, launch of City of Culture year, the gateway and backdrop to Radio 1’s big weekend and several charity events such as Brides across the Bridge. To date, the Peace Bridge has had more than three million people cross it.

The Dark Hedges (Ballymoney-Antrim)

This picturesque lane of beech trees was original established by the Stuart family in the early 18th century.

It was constructed as the showpiece of Gracehill House Garden, a Georgian mansion. The trees continue to be a popular photographic destination in Northern Ireland after they were first planted. As such, the trees have been used as a stand-in for the Kings Road in HBO’s epic fantasy series Game of Thrones.

We wish you a wonderful day in this stunning setting, the Dark Hedges.

Rossnowlagh Beach (Donegal)

Only eight kilometres south of Donegal Town is the beautiful Rossnowlagh beach, with its soft sand and picturesque views across Donegal Bay. Its length is roughly four kilometres (2.5 miles). Rossnowlagh is well-liked by members of the public. The water is suitable for swimming specialists and surfers' experts and the beach is excellent for extended strolls.

The settlement of Rossnowlagh was given the name “Ros Neamlach,” which translates to “heavenly headland” in Irish.

At low tide, you may see the rocky outcrop of Carrickfad, which protrudes from the headland and is located a short distance from the neighbouring beach.

The Foundation for Environmental Education has designated Rossnowlagh as a Blue Flag beach. To maintain this “gold standard,” beaches must be free of any discharges related to industry, wastewater, or sewage.

In the warmer months, locals, and tourists enjoy travelling to the beach to surf, swim, walk and relax on the soft, goldensands while taking in the breath-taking scenery.

Rock of Cashel (Tipperary

The Rock of Cashel, which is famous for its historical importance and is located on a magnificent outcrop of limestone in the Golden Vale, is home to the most impressive cluster of mediaeval buildings that can be found anywhere in Ireland. A round tower, a High Cross, a Romanesque chapel, a Gothic cathedral, an abbey, the Hall of the Vicars Choral and a Tower House from the fourteen century are all included in the list of monuments that can be found here.

Historically, this location served as the seat of the kings of Munster; legend has it that Saint Patrick himself travelled here in an effort to convert King Aenghus to Christianity. Cashel became Brian Boru’s capital after he was appointed High King there in the year 978 and made it his seat of government.

The buildings that have been preserved are quite remarkable. For example, the frescoes at Cormac’s Chapel are the only Romanesque frescoes that have been preserved in Ireland.

One of the most impressive landmarks in all of Ireland the Rock of Cashel is also one of the country’s most popular tourist destinations.
